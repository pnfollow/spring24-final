Nebulae

Nebulae are described as being the birthplace of stars, or star nurseries, because baby stars will form inside them from the condensed gasses. There are 5 main classifications of nebulae: Emission nebulae, reflection nebulae, dark nebulae, planetary nebulae and supernova remnants. The death of a star can also cause the beginning of a nebula, such as supernova remnants and planetary nebulae. Emmision nebulae when nearby radiation from hot stars energize the ionized gasses. Dark nebulae form by dust in the densest parts of molecular clouds, while reflection nebulae form when light from a neighbor dust cloud reflects it stars light onto it.

Galaxies

Galaxies are star systems with supermassive black holes in their centers. There are four main classifications of galaxies: The spiral galaxy with it's spiraling arms, elliptical with it's elliptoid shape, lenticular with am outer ring but no arms, and irregular for not fitting under any of these categories. Spiral galaxies have two different sub-types, being barred and unbarred where barred means the galaxy's arms have a bar that connectes the arms through its central bulge.

Planets

There are 8 planets in our solar system, with some dwarf planets in the Kuiper belt such as Pluto. The reason the planets are in the order they are is thanks to the Sun's solar wind.

Stars

Stars are luminous balls of hot gas that follow a sequence throughout their life cycle that can determine what type of star they’ll turn out to be or how the’ll inevitably explode. There are six main types of stars: main sequence stars, red giants, white dwarfs, neutron stars, red dwarfs, and brown dwarfs.
